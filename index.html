The Twist

Radio Changes. You Adapt.

No two games are the same. These are living, breathing markets. What works in 1973 may fail by 1983. The audience and business are changing all the time.

1970s

AM still matters

Top 40 stations battle each other for young ears, old-line AM stations still get big audiences.

1980s

FM Takes Over

Cars get FM radios and that’s where people want to hear music. How will AM stay relevant?

1990s

Radio Consolidates

Ownership rules change, clusters rise, strategy becomes more ruthless.

2000s+

New pressures

More stations, streaming audio, changing ad markets and shifting audience habits force hard choices.
